Case study method

Explain work in a way non-engineers can follow

Strong case studies should make the value of technical work legible to hiring managers, clients, and internal decision-makers.

Situation

Start with the problem, risk, or operational pain point.

Technical path

Show the diagnosis, design choices, and implementation steps in plain language.

Result

End with the business impact, operational improvement, or lesson learned.

Tone

Professional, not dramatic

These writeups should feel grounded and credible rather than exaggerated. The point is to look trustworthy, not flashy.

Format

Use screenshots and diagrams

When available, pair written explanation with visuals so the page feels more evidence-based and easier to skim.
