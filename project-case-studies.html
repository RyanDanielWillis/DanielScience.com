Evidence structure

How projects should be evaluated.

A project is strongest when the problem, approach, output, and result are easy to understand. That structure helps both technical reviewers and non-technical stakeholders see the value.

Problem

What needed to be solved and why it mattered to users, operations, or the business.

Approach

How the system was analyzed, designed, secured, automated, or implemented.

Result

What became clearer, faster, safer, more reliable, or easier to maintain.
