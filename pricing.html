Approach & rates

Different technical engagements vary a lot in scope. A remote advisory conversation, a network cleanup project, a VoIP remediation effort, and an internal tooling build do not belong in the same fixed package, so a scoped conversation leads to a more accurate estimate and a better fit for the work.

Pricing philosophy

Conversation-first quoting allows us to solve real problems at a reasonable price.

Advisory

Assessment, review, and planning

Best for audits, planning calls, architecture review, migration preparation, environment review, and technical second opinions before a team commits resources.

Project work

Implementation and remediation work

Best for hands-on work such as environment cleanup, deployment assistance, network changes, VoIP remediation, reporting improvements, or building a focused internal tool.

Ongoing support

Ongoing technical support

Best for organizations that need recurring access to technical support, engineering help, or implementation follow-through without hiring a full-time resource immediately.

Why rates are scoped instead of fixed

A one-hour technical review is very different from a multi-step remediation project or after-hours implementation window. Scoping first keeps expectations clearer and pricing more realistic.

Best fit

This approach works well for targeted projects, overflow support, operational cleanup, and relationship-based consulting where clarity and trust matter more than flashy presentation.
