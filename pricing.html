How to engage

Start wherever you are. Scale to what you need.

Every engagement begins with a conversation. From there, the work is shaped to match the problem — whether that's a one-time fix, a major project, or a long-term technical partnership.

Assess & advise

Before committing budget, get a clear picture of where things stand. Architecture reviews, security posture assessments, migration planning, or a plain-English second opinion on a technical decision your business depends on.

Build & remediate

Hands-on delivery: software development, infrastructure cleanup, cybersecurity hardening, automation, and deployment. Work that eliminates the problem — not workarounds that push it to next quarter.

Own & operate

Ongoing technical leadership without a full-time hire. Ryan runs your technical estate — monitoring, vendor management, incident response, and strategic decisions — so nothing falls through the cracks.

How pricing works

No retainer packages. No surprise overruns. Priced to the work.

Pricing is scoped per engagement — because a two-hour security review, a six-week software project, and an embedded technical leadership role are fundamentally different commitments. A short conversation is all it takes to produce an accurate number.

What to bring to the conversation

  • The business outcome you're trying to reach
  • Timeline and any hard deadlines
  • What's broken, missing, or at risk right now
  • Whether this is a one-time engagement, a project, or ongoing work

Who this is built for

Business owners and executives who want one trusted person to own the technical side of their organization. Hiring managers who need someone who can lead, not just execute. Teams that have outgrown their current setup and need a real upgrade — not another band-aid.
